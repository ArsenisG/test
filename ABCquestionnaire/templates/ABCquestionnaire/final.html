Academic Behavioural Confidence

  • The 17 questions you filled in were intended to try and describe dimensions of Academic Behavioural Confidence (ABC)
    (related to the self-concept and self-efficacy of students)
  • The identified four dimensions of ABC, are:
    • Grades-Grd ("Confidence in the actual activity contributing directly to what a student is graded on, including coursework and exams")
    • Verbalising-Vrb ("Confidence in the ability to verbalise their knowledge during their learning activity")
    • Attendance-Att ("Confidence in the willingness to appropriately attend events during their learning activity")
    • Studying-Std ("Confidence in undertaking the self-orginised activity necessary for their learning experience")
  • The results of your 17 responses are shown in the below constellation graph plot (different number of the questions relate
    to the different dimensions) (each line of points joins the responses from different sets of questions).

  • The base line of the constellation graph shows the index values of different confidence dimensions, over the consistent domain 0.0
    (low confidence) to 1.0 (high confidence).
